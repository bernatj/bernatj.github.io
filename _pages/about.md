I am a "César Nombela" Postdoctoral Fellow at the [Instituto de Geociencias (IGEO)](https://igeo.ucm-csic.es/), a joint center of the Universidad Complutense de Madrid (UCM) and the Consejo Superior de Investigaciones Científicas (CSIC).

My research focuses on atmospheric dynamics and climate variability, with particular interest in heatwaves, climate extremes, teleconnections, and sub-seasonal-to-seasonal (S2S) predictability. I study how remote drivers such as El Niño–Southern Oscillation (ENSO) and the stratospheric polar vortex influence seasonal forecasts over Europe and beyond.

I combine observational datasets and reanalysis products with numerical weather prediction models, general circulation models, and emerging machine learning techniques to better understand and predict high-impact weather events.
